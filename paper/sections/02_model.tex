\section{Model and methods}
\label{sec:model}

\subsection{Joint spectral amplitude}

We consider collinear, single-spatial-mode SPDC and describe the emitted
photon pair by its joint spectral amplitude (JSA). Writing $\nu_s$ and
$\nu_i$ for the angular-frequency detunings of signal and idler from
their central design frequencies, the JSA factorizes into an
energy-conservation term set by the pump and a momentum-conservation
term set by the crystal \cite{grice1997},
\begin{equation}
  f(\nu_s,\nu_i) = \alpha(\nu_s+\nu_i)\,\phi(\nu_s,\nu_i) .
  \label{eq:jsa}
\end{equation}
For a transform-limited Gaussian pump the envelope is
$\alpha(\nu) = \exp[-\nu^2/(2\sigma^2)]$, a band along the antidiagonal
$\nu_s+\nu_i=0$; $\sigma$ is the standard deviation of the amplitude
envelope in angular frequency. Bandwidths are quoted throughout as the
corresponding ordinary-frequency values; the reference pump has
$\sigma/2\pi = 0.164$~THz. For the phase matching we use the standard
first-order group-velocity-mismatch (GVM) expansion of the wave-vector
mismatch in a crystal of length $L$,
\begin{equation}
  \phi(\nu_s,\nu_i) = \mathrm{sinc}\!\left(\frac{\Delta k\,L}{2}\right),
  \qquad
  \Delta k = \kappa_s \nu_s + \kappa_i \nu_i ,
  \label{eq:pm}
\end{equation}
with $\mathrm{sinc}(x)=\sin(x)/x$ and
$\kappa_j = 1/v_g^{\mathrm{pump}} - 1/v_g^{(j)}$ the group-velocity
mismatch between the pump and photon $j$. The reference crystal has
$L = 10$~mm, $\kappa_s = +0.207$~ns/m, and $\kappa_i = -0.318$~ns/m,
representative of PPKTP pumped at 775~nm and producing degenerate pairs
at 1550~nm \cite{grice1997,uren2005,graffitti2018}. The ratio
$\kappa_s/\kappa_i$ sets the tilt of the sinc ridge in the
$(\nu_s,\nu_i)$ plane, and the interplay of this tilt with the pump
band determines how separable the JSA can be
(Fig.~\ref{fig:anatomy}). We deliberately retain only this first-order
model: no Sellmeier dispersion, no higher-order terms, and no
apodization of the nonlinearity \cite{branczyk2011,graffitti2018}, so
that the imperfection budgets below isolate pump effects at a fixed,
transparent phase-matching geometry.

\subsection{Schmidt decomposition}

The JSA is evaluated on a square grid of $n$ points per axis over a
window of half-width 4--8~THz (chosen per study so that the intensity
decays to zero well inside the boundary) and normalized so that
$\sum |f|^2 = 1$. The Schmidt decomposition of the discretized JSA is
its singular-value decomposition \cite{law2000}: with singular values
$s_k$, the Schmidt coefficients are
$\lambda_k = s_k^2 / \sum_j s_j^2$, and the heralded-photon spectral
purity and Schmidt number are
\begin{equation}
  P = \sum_k \lambda_k^2 , \qquad K = 1/P .
  \label{eq:schmidt}
\end{equation}
$P = 1$ ($K = 1$) corresponds to a fully separable JSA. At the
reference point $P = 0.8100$ ($K = 1.2345$).

\subsection{Imperfect pump as a mixed-state ensemble}

An imperfect pump is a classical ensemble of pure pump realizations,
so the heralded signal photon is described by a density matrix rather
than a single Schmidt spectrum. We Monte-Carlo sample $N$ pure
realizations per sweep point ($N = 120$ in the standard sweeps,
$N = 240$ in the fine-resolution jitter study, and $N = 1000$ in the
RIN sweep, whose per-realization cost is lower), compute the
discretized JSA matrix $A_k$ for each, and accumulate
\begin{equation}
  \rho \propto \sum_{k} w_k\, A_k A_k^\dagger ,
  \label{eq:rho}
\end{equation}
from which the heralded purity follows in a normalization-independent
form,
\begin{equation}
  P = \frac{\mathrm{Tr}(\rho^2)}{[\mathrm{Tr}(\rho)]^2} .
  \label{eq:purity}
\end{equation}
Each imperfection is parameterized directly by laser datasheet
statistics and enters only the pump. Per realization we sample:
(i) \emph{center jitter/drift} --- a Gaussian shift of the pump center
frequency with the quoted rms value, treated as a quasi-static
shot-to-shot ensemble.

This quasi-static treatment has a definite regime of validity. The
pump is \emph{pulsed}: $\sigma/2\pi = 0.164$~THz corresponds to
picosecond-class pulses, and RIN in this model is pulse-energy noise.
The ensemble picture holds for noise processes slower than the pump
coherence time, $\tau_{\mathrm{coh}} \sim 1/\sigma \approx 1$~ps, so
that each pair sees a static pump center, and faster than the
integration time, so that the ensemble is fully sampled. For an
80~MHz-class source integrated over seconds this window spans noise
frequencies from $\sim$Hz to tens of GHz and covers the dominant
laboratory processes --- thermal drift, acoustic and mount vibrations,
and drive-current noise. Sub-picosecond noise components instead act
coherently on each pure JSA and lie outside the model.

(ii) \emph{Longitudinal multimode structure} ---
one comb line drawn from a set of $M$ discrete lines centered
symmetrically on the pump, at offsets $(m - (M-1)/2)$ times the mode
spacing for $m = 0,\dots,M-1$ (half-integer multiples of the spacing
when $M$ is even), with normalized line weights (uniform unless
stated); the total pump shift is the sum of
the comb-line offset and the jitter draw. Drawing a single line per
realization asserts that the longitudinal modes are mutually
\emph{incoherent} --- the correct description of a free-running
multimode diode, whose modes carry random relative phases and exhibit
mode-partition noise. It is not the correct description of a
phase-coherent mode-locked comb: there the JSA is a coherent sum over
the lines --- a pure state, not a mixture --- and the comb teeth,
spaced by an 80~MHz-class repetition rate, are in any case far too
fine to be resolved by the JSA.

(iii) \emph{Relative
intensity noise} (RIN) --- a realization weight
$w = \max[0,\, 1 + \mathcal{N}(0, r^2)]$, where $\mathcal{N}(0, r^2)$
is a zero-mean Gaussian with standard deviation $r$, the fractional rms
intensity noise, each realization's JSA being individually normalized.

Every Monte-Carlo sweep behind the figures and the budgets of
Table~\ref{tab:budget} is run with $S = 16$ independent seeds (the
RIN sweep, whose heralded purity is exactly independent of the sampled
weights --- see the validation suite below --- uses $S = 8$).
Auxiliary convergence checks --- the single-seed grid-refinement
replicas described below and the eight-seed $N$-doubling check of the
jitter budgets --- use fewer seeds. Each
(seed, point) pair draws its $N$ realizations from an independent
random stream seeded by the pair itself; seeding streams by seed plus
point index would instead make replicas overlap (seed $s+1$ at sweep
point $i$ reproducing seed $s$ at point $i+1$) and thereby correlate
nominally independent replicas. Reported curves are means over the $S$
per-seed curves, and the imperfection budgets of
Table~\ref{tab:budget} are quoted as the mean $\pm$ standard error of
the mean (SEM) over the per-seed threshold crossings.

\subsection{Closed-form benchmark: the double-Gaussian model}

The Monte-Carlo machinery can be checked against mathematics rather
than against itself. Replacing the sinc in Eq.~(\ref{eq:pm}) by a
Gaussian phase-matching function \cite{grice1997,graffitti2018},
\begin{equation}
  \phi_G(\nu_s,\nu_i)
  = \exp\!\left[-\gamma \left(\frac{\Delta k\, L}{2}\right)^{\!2}\right],
  \qquad \gamma = 0.193,
  \label{eq:phig}
\end{equation}
makes the JSA a two-dimensional Gaussian and every quantity of
interest analytic. The value of $\gamma$ is fixed by matching the
\emph{amplitude} half-maximum of the sinc: both functions equal $1/2$
at $x = 1.8955$. Note that because the crystal amplitude is
$\exp(-\gamma x^2)$, the curvature entering the quadratic form below
is $2\gamma$. With $a_j = \kappa_j L/2$, the JSA is
$f_G \propto \exp\{-\tfrac{1}{2}[A\nu_s^2 + 2C\nu_s\nu_i +
B\nu_i^2]\}$ with
\[
  A = \frac{1}{\sigma^2} + 2\gamma a_s^2 , \qquad
  B = \frac{1}{\sigma^2} + 2\gamma a_i^2 , \qquad
  C = \frac{1}{\sigma^2} + 2\gamma a_s a_i ,
\]
and tracing out one photon gives the pure-state purity in closed form,
\begin{equation}
  P_0 = \sqrt{1 - \frac{C^2}{A B}} .
  \label{eq:p0}
\end{equation}
For Gaussian center jitter of rms $J$ (ordinary Hz) the mixed purity
of Eq.~(\ref{eq:purity}) also closes:
\begin{equation}
  P(J) = \frac{P_0}{\sqrt{1 + 4 c\,(2\pi J)^2}} ,
  \qquad
  c = \frac{1}{2\sigma^2 + 1/(\gamma a_s^2)} .
  \label{eq:pj}
\end{equation}
Three points in the derivation are easy to get wrong. First, the
cross term required for $\mathrm{Tr}(\rho^2)$ is
$\mathrm{Tr}(A A^\dagger A' A'^\dagger) = \| A^\dagger A' \|_F^2$,
not the two-photon overlap $|\langle f | f' \rangle|^2$, which is the
purity of the mixed \emph{two-photon} state --- a different quantity.
Second, mixed reduced states are signal/idler \emph{asymmetric}: the
constant $c$ carries the GVM constant of the \emph{traced-out} photon,
and since our $\rho$ in Eq.~(\ref{eq:rho}) keeps the idler, $c$
contains $a_s$. Third, a pump-center shift is an exact rigid
translation of the JSA, so per-realization normalization is exact and
the pair rate is independent of jitter in this model. At the reference
point Eq.~(\ref{eq:p0}) gives $P_0 = 0.9818$ --- intrinsically more
separable than the sinc model's $P = 0.8100$, whose side lobes carry
entanglement --- and exact separability ($C = 0$, $P_0 = 1$) occurs at
a 0.1997~THz pump bandwidth, possible because
$\kappa_s \kappa_i < 0$. Section~\ref{sec:results} uses
Eqs.~(\ref{eq:p0}) and (\ref{eq:pj}) as an external validation of the
full Monte-Carlo pipeline (Fig.~\ref{fig:validation}).

\subsection{From datasheet units to model parameters}

Manufacturers publish spectral widths in MHz or nm, stabilities in pm
or pm/K, RIN as a dB/Hz spectral density, and cavity geometry in mm
--- none of which the model accepts directly. The translation layer is
deliberately explicit; each conversion carries one stated physical
assumption. A Gaussian intensity-spectrum FWHM
$\Delta\nu_{\mathrm{FWHM}}$ maps to the model bandwidth (the standard
deviation of the amplitude envelope) as
\begin{equation}
  \frac{\sigma}{2\pi} = \frac{\Delta\nu_{\mathrm{FWHM}}}{2\sqrt{\ln 2}} .
  \label{eq:fwhm}
\end{equation}
Wavelength excursions convert at first order via
\begin{equation}
  \delta\nu = \frac{c\,\delta\lambda}{\lambda^2} ,
  \label{eq:dlam}
\end{equation}
i.e.\ 499.1~GHz per nm at $\lambda = 775$~nm. A bounded drift
specification (``$\pm a$ over $T$ hours'', or a pm/K coefficient times
a temperature stability) defines a peak-to-peak excursion band
$\mathrm{pp} = 2a$ rather than a Gaussian; taking the center uniformly
distributed over the band gives
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{rms} = \frac{\mathrm{pp}}{2\sqrt{3}} .
  \label{eq:pp}
\end{equation}
A RIN spectral density, assumed white over the collection bandwidth
$B$ (the inverse of the detection gate or pulse period), integrates to
the fractional rms
\begin{equation}
  r = \left[ 10^{\mathrm{RIN}/10}\, B \right]^{1/2} ,
  \label{eq:rinint}
\end{equation}
and a standing-wave laser cavity of length $L_{\mathrm{cav}}$ and
refractive index $n$ has longitudinal-mode spacing
\begin{equation}
  \mathrm{FSR} = \frac{c}{2 n L_{\mathrm{cav}}} ,
  \label{eq:fsr}
\end{equation}
which maps onto the comb spacing of the multimode model.

One published number deserves a caveat rather than a conversion: a
Lorentzian linewidth arises from fast phase diffusion and has no
finite second moment, so no rms exists and the quasi-static ensemble
does not describe it --- phase noise that decorrelates within a pulse
does not rigidly displace the pump band. We therefore use the
Lorentzian HWHM only as a deliberately conservative stand-in bound on
the center jitter; for the MHz-class linewidths of DFB diodes this
bound sits orders of magnitude below the GHz-scale budgets of
Table~\ref{tab:budget} and is negligible. Slow frequency-drift
specifications ($\pm$pm over hours, pm/K times a temperature
stability) \emph{are} quasi-static and enter through
Eqs.~(\ref{eq:dlam}) and (\ref{eq:pp}) instead. These conversions are
applied to real products in Table~\ref{tab:datasheet}.

\subsection{Numerical robustness and validation}

Ideal-pump sweeps use $n = 512$; Monte-Carlo studies use $n = 256$,
which is converged to better than $10^{-4}$ in $P$ against $n = 768$
at the hardest points of the parameter sweeps (0.05~THz bandwidth over
a 6~THz half-window; 2~mm crystal over an 8~THz half-window). The RIN
sweep uses a coarser $n = 64$ grid: there every realization shares the
identical JSA and only the scalar weights vary, so the reported purity
invariance is exact at any grid size. Single-seed replicas of the
10~mm jitter sweep at $n = 128$ and $n = 192$ shift the mixed purity
by only $\sim 2\times10^{-5}$, far below the seed-to-seed Monte-Carlo
spread: sampling noise, not discretization, dominates the error
budget. The code ships with a validation suite of fourteen physics
tests, including exact collapse of the zero-imperfection ensemble to
the pure-state SVD purity ($P = 0.8100$), exact RIN invariance of the
heralded purity (difference below $10^{-9}$), reduction of a comb with
zero side-line weight to the single-mode result, monotonic degradation
of purity with jitter, and closed-form benchmarks against the
double-Gaussian model: the SVD purity matches Eq.~(\ref{eq:p0}) to
better than $10^{-4}$, and the full Monte-Carlo pipeline reproduces
Eq.~(\ref{eq:pj}) including its finite-$N$ estimator bias.
