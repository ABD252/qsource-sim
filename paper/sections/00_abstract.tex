Spectrally pure heralded single photons from spontaneous parametric
down-conversion (SPDC) are usually engineered assuming an ideal pump
laser. We numerically translate three quantities printed on any
pump-laser datasheet -- center-frequency jitter and drift, relative
intensity noise (RIN), and longitudinal-mode structure -- into a
quantitative budget for the heralded spectral purity. Using a
first-order group-velocity-mismatch model of a 10 mm PPKTP-like source
(ideal purity 0.810), each imperfection enters as a Monte-Carlo
ensemble of pure pump realizations, and the purity is evaluated from
the heralded photon's ensemble density matrix. Center jitter of 26, 61,
and 88 GHz rms consumes a 1\%, 5\%, and 10\% relative purity budget,
respectively; a re-matched 5 mm crystal roughly doubles these
tolerances. Even one additional equal-power longitudinal mode at 0.25
THz spacing exceeds the entire 10\% budget. RIN, by contrast, leaves the purity
exactly invariant but inflates accidental double-pair emission as
$1+r^2$. Datasheet lines thus map onto two distinct damage channels:
spectral (jitter, mode structure) and statistical (RIN).
