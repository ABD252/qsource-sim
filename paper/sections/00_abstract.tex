Spectrally pure heralded single photons from spontaneous parametric
down-conversion (SPDC) are usually engineered assuming an ideal pump
laser. We translate three pump-laser datasheet quantities --
center-frequency jitter, relative intensity noise (RIN), and
longitudinal-mode structure -- into a quantitative budget for
heralded spectral purity. In a first-order group-velocity-mismatch
model of a 10 mm PPKTP-like source, each imperfection enters as a
Monte-Carlo ensemble of pure pump realizations whose averaged density
matrix yields the heralded purity. The machinery is validated
against an exact closed form for Gaussian phase matching,
agreeing within error bars. Center jitter of $27.7 \pm 0.3$,
$62.7 \pm 0.7$, and $91 \pm 1$ GHz rms consumes a 1\%, 5\%, and 10\%
relative purity budget; budgets scale as $1/L$, doubling for a
re-matched 5 mm crystal and halving at 20 mm. One extra equal-power
longitudinal mode at 0.25 THz spacing exceeds the 10\% budget.
RIN leaves the purity exactly invariant but inflates accidental
double-pair emission as $1+r^2$. An explicit translation layer maps
real datasheet lines -- linewidth, $d\lambda/dT$, RIN in dB/Hz,
cavity free spectral range -- onto this budget for three commercial
laser classes.
